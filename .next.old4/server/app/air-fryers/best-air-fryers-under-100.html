Best Air Fryers Under $100 in 2026

Compare the best air fryers under $100. See what you gain and sacrifice at this price point.

## Introduction You can get a solid air fryer for under $100. At this price, you're buying reliable performance, good capacity, and proven brands. You'll sacrifice premium features like app control or multiple zones, but you won't sacrifice crisp, even cooking. This guide compares the best options under $100. ## The Budget Tier Sweet Spot Under $100, you can expect: 3-6 quart capacity, 1500-1750 wattage, 400F max temperature, and 6-10 preset functions. Most are basket-style (not oven-style). Build quality varies, but all models here are proven performers. ## Best Options Under $100 ### Cosori Pro LE - $80 (Best Overall Value) The Pro LE is the gold standard of budget air fryers. It's compact (5 quarts), reliable, and includes 11 preset functions that actually work well. **Specs**: 5 quarts, 1500W, 400F max, 11 presets, compact design. **What you get**: Quiet operation, even crisping, simple one-button controls, compact design perfect for apartments. The basket is dishwasher-safe. Build quality is solid for the price. **What you sacrifice**: No dual zone, no oven functions, basic display (not digital timer), smaller capacity than models $20 higher. **Best for**: Apartment dwellers, singles, couples, or anyone wanting reliable budget performance. This is the safest budget choice. **Our rating**: 4.0/5 ### CHEFMAN TurboFry - $40 (Ultra-Budget) If $80 feels high, the CHEFMAN hits $40. It's tiny (3.6 quarts) but genuinely works for one person. **Specs**: 3.6 quarts, 1400W, 380F max, 6 presets, very compact. **What you get**: Super-affordable entry point. Heats quickly. Works as advertised. Good for dorms or RVs. **What you sacrifice**: Very small (single or double serving only). Limited features. Basic construction. Won't satisfy families or batch-cooking needs. **Best for**: Ultra-budget shoppers, singles, dorm rooms, trial users testing if they like air frying. **Our rating**: 3.9/5 ### Cosori TurboBlaze - $100 (Best Capacity at $100) The TurboBlaze is Cosori's entry into the $100 space. It offers more capacity (6 quarts) than the Pro LE while staying at $100. **Specs**: 6 quarts, 1700W, 400F max, 10 presets, sleek design. **What you get**: Larger capacity than Pro LE, 10 preset modes, good heating element, intuitive controls. Mid-size is ideal for small families. **What you sacrifice**: Less proven track record than Pro LE. Average build quality. Not as compact as Pro LE. **Best for**: Small families (2-4 people), those wanting mid-size capacity, users comfortable with Cosori brand. **Our rating**: 4.1/5 ### Instant Vortex Plus - $100 (Most Reliable Brand) Instant is known for reliability (their Instant Pot pedigree). The Vortex Plus delivers solid performance at $100. **Specs**: 6 quarts, 1700W, 400F max, 6 presets, sturdy build. **What you get**: Proven reliability, sturdy construction, crisps evenly, good warranty (2 years). Trustworthy brand with good support. **What you sacrifice**: Fewer presets than competitors (only 6), basic interface, less feature-rich than Cosori options. **Best for**: Reliability-focused buyers, those who prefer fewer buttons and simpler operation, fans of Instant Pot ecosystem. **Our rating**: 4.2/5 ### Ninja Foodi XL - $130 (Just Over Budget, Worth It) Technically $30 over the threshold, but worth mentioning. Ninja's quality jumps noticeably here. **Specs**: 5.5 quarts, 1750W, 400F max, 8 presets, Ninja reliability. **What you get**: Ninja's build quality and engineering, 8 presets (well-tuned), quick heat-up, proven track record. Step up in reliability and features. **What you sacrifice**: Not under $100 (though often on sale). **Best for**: Those willing to stretch budget for Ninja quality. **Our rating**: 4.2/5 ## What You're Actually Sacrificing Under $100 At this price, you're not getting: - **Dual zone cooking** - that requires $110+ (Ninja DualZone AF300) - **App control** - only premium models ($200+) - **Rotisserie** - oven-style feature, not in baskets - **Oven functions** - baking, toasting, roasting require $150+ - **Digital displays** - most use dial/button controls - **Premium build** - feel is plastic, not stainless steel You ARE getting: - **Solid crisping** - all these models crisp evenly - **Reliable operation** - proven brands and track records - **Adequate capacity** - 3.6-6 quarts covers most meals - **Good speed** - air frying is still much faster than conventional ovens - **Low noise** - under-$100 models often run quiet ## What You Spend Extra On Above $100 Once you cross $100: - **$110-$120**: Dual zone models (two baskets, same unit) - **$150-$200**: Oven-style units (add baking, toasting) - **$200+**: Premium brands (Breville, Philips) with smart features - **$250+**: Full-featured systems with app control ## Real-World Performance All these under-$100 models will cook fries in 15 minutes, chicken wings in 12 minutes, and chicken breasts in 18 minutes. Speed differences are minimal. Crisping quality is good across the board. The difference is in durability (how long the basket lasts), noise level, and interface friendliness. Cosori and Instant tend to outlast budget competitors. ## Buying Recommendation **Best overall**: Cosori Pro LE at $80 - safest choice, proven performance, great value. **Best value for families**: Cosori TurboBlaze at $100 - larger capacity, more presets, still affordable. **Best budget option**: CHEFMAN TurboFry at $40 - if you only need occasional air frying. **Best reliability**: Instant Vortex Plus at $100 - proven brand, solid warranty. Don't overthink this. At under $100, all models work well. Pick the capacity (3.6, 5, or 6 quarts) that fits your household, and pick the brand you trust. You won't regret the purchase. ## Conclusion For under $100, you're buying proven basket-style air fryers with good crisping, reliable performance, and adequate capacity. Budget is genuinely sufficient for quality here. The Cosori Pro LE at $80 is the sweet spot: reliability, good reviews, proven performance, and excellent value. Unless you need dual zones or oven functions (which require spending more), under-$100 models are absolutely worth buying.
